# Content Pillars

## About

Define the main thematic clusters that organize your content strategy. Every new piece of content should fit into one of these pillars or trigger a discussion about creating a new one.

Pillars prevent thematic drift and help AI agents understand which topics are in-scope vs. out-of-scope.

## Schema

Each pillar must have:

- **Name** (required): 2–4 words, memorable, distinct from others
- **Purpose** (required): One sentence — what outcome does this pillar drive?
- **What fits** (required): Bullet list of 2–3 examples (what content goes here)
- **What doesn't** (optional): Bullet list of 1–2 counterexamples (what doesn't belong)
- **Owner** (optional): Who decides scope for this pillar

**Rules:**
- Pillars must not overlap. If content could fit two pillars equally, the pillar definition is too vague — narrow it.
- When new content doesn't fit any pillar, default is to discuss and add a new one, not stretch an existing definition.

## Content Pillars

### Example: Thought Leadership

**Purpose:** Establish credibility through original research and opinions.

**What fits:**
- Original analysis or frameworks
- Contrarian takes on industry trends
- Behind-the-scenes decision-making stories

**What doesn't:**
- News aggregation or link roundups
- How-tos for mainstream products

---

### Example: Practical Guides

**Purpose:** Help users solve specific problems with step-by-step instructions.

**What fits:**
- Tutorials and walkthroughs
- Checklists and templates
- Troubleshooting guides

**What doesn't:**
- Conceptual explanations without actionable steps
- Product announcements

---

## Adding a New Pillar

Before adding a pillar, ask:
1. Do we have 3+ pieces of content that fit this and nothing else?
2. Does it support the project's main job (see `PROJECT.md`)?
3. Does it avoid overlapping with existing pillars?

If yes to all three, add it using the schema above.
